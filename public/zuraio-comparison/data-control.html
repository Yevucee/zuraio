Data control

Keep control of company data while using AI.

Zuraio AI Hub is designed for companies that want AI benefits without uncontrolled data exposure, unclear model routing or generic external processing.

Why it matters

Company knowledge is more than information.

Company knowledge includes client context, pricing, negotiations, internal decisions, project history, employee knowledge and commercial judgement. Once that knowledge enters uncontrolled tools, companies may lose visibility over where it goes and how it is used.

Data ownership

Your data stays yours.

Zuraio is designed so that company data and knowledge remain under company control. The specific ownership, retention and processing terms depend on your agreed deployment model and contract.

Company retains ownership

Your documents, emails, business records and internal knowledge remain your property. Zuraio is intended to process data on your behalf within agreed boundaries — not to claim ownership of your content.

No training on your data by default

Zuraio is designed so that your company data is not used to train public models.

Access control

Access should follow company rules.

Zuraio is designed around permissions, roles and approved data sources so that AI assistance reflects the company's access model — not a separate, unrestricted layer.

Role-based permissions

Access can be structured around roles, users and approved data sources. Users should only see what they are permitted to see in connected systems.

Source approval

Administrators can define which knowledge sources are connected and available to which users or teams.

Model routing by policy

Requests may be routed to different models depending on sensitivity, task type and agreed policy.

Encryption and security

Security designed for business data.

Security measures depend on the chosen deployment model. The following are design intentions — specific certifications and technical details must be verified before publication.

Encryption in transit

Designed to encrypt data in transit between users, the AI Hub and connected systems.

Encryption at rest

Designed to protect stored data according to the agreed deployment and storage configuration.

Audit and logging

Requests, model calls and source usage are designed to be logged according to the agreed deployment and audit model.

Swiss and EU expectations

Built with Swiss and European data-control expectations in mind. Specific compliance claims require legal verification.

Six questions

Questions before using AI with company data.

  1. Where is our data processed?
  2. Can we decide which model handles which task?
  3. Can users only access what they are allowed to access?
  4. Can we review which sources were used?
  5. Can we prevent sensitive data from going to the wrong place?
  6. Can the system adapt to our internal processes?
