Knowledge

Your company knowledge, connected and searchable.

Zuraio is designed to connect approved internal sources so employees can ask questions and receive answers backed by the documents and systems your company already uses.

What can be connected

Approved sources for company-specific answers.

The right sources depend on your organisation, permissions and agreed setup. Zuraio is designed to work with selected internal knowledge — not public internet data alone.

Documents and files

Policies, standards, proposals, project files, templates and shared folders from approved storage locations.

Examples may include SharePoint, OneDrive, Google Drive — subject to integration availability.

Email and calendar

Relevant threads, meeting context and correspondence where access permissions allow — for briefings, follow-ups and search.

Examples may include Outlook, Gmail — subject to integration availability.

Business systems

CRM records, ERP data, project tools and other approved systems that hold operational context.

See integrations for current status by category.

Meeting notes and transcripts

Notes, summaries and recorded context from meetings — where your company chooses to connect them.

Internal wikis and knowledge bases

Structured internal documentation, FAQs and team knowledge repositories.

Custom and API sources

Additional sources via APIs or custom connectors, depending on your deployment and requirements.

Sourced answers

Answers you can trace back to a source.

Generic AI can sound confident without showing where information came from. Zuraio is designed so that, where internal sources are used, users can see references to the documents or systems behind an answer — making it easier to check, share and trust.

Why sources matter

  • Verify claims before acting on them
  • Share answers with colleagues who need the original context
  • Reduce reliance on unverified model memory
  • Support internal review and governance expectations

What users may see

  • Document name and location (where permissions allow)
  • Relevant excerpt or reference pointer
  • Date or version indicator where available
  • Indication when an answer is not source-backed
